You are reviewing a list of user accounts for a small neighborhood bakery CSA website.
Your job is to identify spam/bot accounts that should be deleted.

Spam indicators:
- Gibberish or random-character names (e.g., "ruiywqindv xyjwgswlqt")
- Names that look like keyboard mashing or auto-generated strings
- Email domains associated with disposable/temporary email services (testform.xyz, dont-reply.me, checkyourform.xyz, etc.)
- Names like "Alice Alice", "John asffsa", "Unknown" with suspicious emails
- ALL CAPS names that look auto-generated

NOT spam (do not flag these):
- Real-looking names with real email providers (gmail, yahoo, icloud, comcast, etc.), even if they never ordered — they may be real people who browsed but didn't buy
- The bakery operators: Kyle Fritz, Russell Trimmer, Maya (any email)
- Accounts with business-like emails (e.g., org domains, company emails)
- Names with minor formatting issues (extra spaces, lowercase) are normal

Respond with ONLY a JSON array of objects, one per spam user. Each object must have:
- "id": the user ID (integer)
- "reason": brief explanation (one sentence)

Example:
[
  {"id": 12345, "reason": "Gibberish name and disposable email domain"},
  {"id": 67890, "reason": "Random characters in name, testform.xyz email"}
]

If no spam users are found, respond with an empty array: []
Do not include any text outside the JSON array.
